Roya Naini (b. 1976, Seattle) grew up in Olympia, Washington and since 2010 resides in Stockholm, Sweden. As a composer she works primarily within the field of electroacoustic music using post-spectralist and counterpoint techniques along with an array of software analysis tools, modular synthesis, and traditional/experimental acoustic instruments. Her compositions often incorporate a sense of drama and heightened tensions that explore lurid themes of the human psyche. She holds a special affinity for stark dynamics and contrasts, influenced heavily by visual arts where light and shadow play important roles.

As an artist and producer, Roya has released five albums of electronica/electronic pop and played extensively live in Sweden and North America. She has also produced sound and remixes for other artists and contributed vocals for live concerts.

Roya is currently studying electroacoustic music composition and pipe organ at the Royal College of Music in Stockholm and also via exchange at the Estonian Academy of Music and Theatre in Tallinn. She holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Sociology from the University of Washington, Seattle.
